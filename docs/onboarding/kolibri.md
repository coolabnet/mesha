# Kolibri — Community Learning Platform

## What is Kolibri?

Kolibri is a free learning platform that brings educational content to your phone or computer without needing internet. It contains lessons, videos, exercises, and quizzes for all ages — from primary school through adult education — and it runs entirely on the community's own server.

---

## How to Access Kolibri

Open a browser on your phone, tablet, or computer and go to:

```text
http://aprendizado.bairro.local
```

You must be connected to the **community WiFi network**. Kolibri does not need internet — it works entirely on the community server.

> **Tip:** If the page does not open, check that you are on the community WiFi. If you are connected and it still does not open, contact the community maintainer.

---

## Logging In

### As a student (registered account)

If the community facilitator has registered you as a student:

1. Open Kolibri in your browser
2. Click **"Sign in"**
3. Enter your username and password
4. Click **"Sign in"**

Your progress and completed exercises are saved to your account each time you log in.

### As a guest (no account needed)

You can also use Kolibri without an account:

1. Open Kolibri in your browser
2. Click **"Explore without account"** or **"Continue as guest"** (if this option is shown)
3. You can browse and view content, but your progress will not be saved

If you want your progress saved, ask the facilitator to create an account for you.

### Forgot your password?

Ask the community facilitator or maintainer to reset your password. They can do this from the Kolibri admin panel.

## Finding and Using Learning Content

### Browsing lessons and channels

After logging in (or as a guest), you will see the home screen with available content.

1. Click on a **channel** (a collection of lessons on a topic — for example, Khan Academy, CK-12, or community-created content)
2. Browse through subjects (Maths, Science, Languages, etc.)
3. Click on a topic or lesson to open it

### Watching a video lesson

1. Find the lesson you want to watch
2. Click on it — the video will play directly in the browser
3. Use the play, pause, and fullscreen controls as you would on any video

### Doing an exercise or quiz

1. Find an exercise (it will have a pencil or question mark icon)
2. Click on it to start
3. Answer the questions — Kolibri will tell you right away if your answer is correct
4. Your score and progress are saved to your account (if you are logged in)

### Searching for a topic

1. Click the **Search** icon (magnifying glass) at the top of the page
2. Type the topic you want to find (for example: "fractions", "photosynthesis", "writing")
3. Browse the results

## For Facilitators: Enrolling Students and Tracking Progress

This section is for teachers, tutors, or anyone managing learning groups in Kolibri.

### Creating a student account

1. Log in with your facilitator or admin account
2. Go to **"Facility"** in the top menu (or the side menu on a small screen)
3. Click **"Users"**
4. Click **"Create new user"**
5. Enter the student's name, a username, and a password
6. Set their role to **"Learner"**
7. Click **"Save"**

### Creating a class or group

1. Go to **"Classes"** in the Facility menu
2. Click **"Create new class"**
3. Give the class a name (for example: "Turma A 2026" or "Adult Literacy Group")
4. Add learners to the class by searching for their usernames
5. Assign lessons or exercises to the class from the content library

### Tracking progress

1. Go to **"Reports"** in the top menu
2. Select a class or an individual learner
3. You can see:
   - Which content they have viewed
   - Which exercises they have completed
   - Their scores on quizzes
   - How much time they have spent learning

This information helps you understand who needs extra support and which topics have been covered.

### Assigning a lesson

1. Go to the class you want to assign work to
2. Click **"Lessons"**
3. Click **"New lesson"**
4. Give it a name and add content from the library
5. Click **"Continue"** — learners in that class will see the lesson on their home screen

## About Content Channels and Downloading

Kolibri shows only the content that has been downloaded to the community server. If you want to add new content (for example, a new subject or a new language):

- A maintainer or admin needs to download the channel from the internet while the server has a connection
- Once downloaded, the content is available to everyone on the community network without internet

If you need specific educational material that is not currently in Kolibri, ask the community maintainer. They can check if it is available to download.

> **Note:** Kolibri has a large library of free educational content available in many languages, including Portuguese. Downloading a full channel can take a long time and uses significant storage space — coordinate with the maintainer before requesting new content.
